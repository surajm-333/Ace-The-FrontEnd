Detail about Vanitas no carte (anime series / TV):

Description:

We are at the end of the 19th century. Vampire Noé travels to Paris in an airship and just witnesses a vampiric rampage! Vampires? Airships and steam engines? According to PANDORAHEARTS, Jun Mochizuki shows her readers an exciting steampunk fantasy world, wonderfully brought to life with her detailed artwork! A world where vampires and humans can coexist, because the modern vampire does not thirst for blood. Only when the curse of the blue moon hits a vampire does it awaken a bloodlust in them! Allegedly there is no way to stop these curse vampires and cure their illness. But then a human doctor by the name of Vanitas appears who, with the help of a book, is able to stop the bloodlust of these vampires! He claims that he inherited the name "Vanitas" and its mystical book and that it is his duty to help the suffering vampires. But is his story true? After all, according to legend, it was the vampire VANITAS who forced the curse on the other vampires with his magic book ...

Synopsis:

End of the 19th century. Paris is in turmoil following repeated attacks by vampires. However, the golden rule of their community is not to attack humans! A mysterious evil seems to eat away at these immortal creatures ... It is during this troubled period that Noah arrives in the capital. Born a bloodsucker, he follows in the footsteps of Vanitas' grimoire, legendary artifact feared by all vampires. It is said that it allows its holder to interfere with what is most sacred to them: the real name, the very symbol of their life. The change can make them crazy or even destroy ... On board the huge floating vessel on which it has embarked, Noah made the acquaintance of Amelia . While he helps her to recover from a malaise, everything takes off: she loses her mind and reveals her nature as a vampire in front of the passengers! That's when a mysterious assailant comes on the scene, posing as ... Vanitas ! Before a Noahspeechless, he unsheathes the famous grimoire and appeases the young woman's fit of madness. Isn't the artifact just a lethal weapon?
